Rich ragu

A rich, versatile meat sauce: serve it Bolognese-fashion with spaghetti or use it as a base for lasagne or moussaka

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, halved and finely chopped
  • 1 celery stick, finely diced
  • 1 large carrot, finely diced
  • 600g % minced red meat steak
  • three tbsp tomato purée
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely grated
  • 2 tsp sparkling thyme leaves
  • 150ml crimson wine (or use more red meat stock)
  • 500ml beef stock
  • 400g spaghetti
  • 50g grated parmesan, plus extra to serve (optional)
  • side salad, to serve

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan and add the onion, celery and carrot. Fry over a medium heat for 10 mins, stirring frequently, till

    softened and the colour of the vegetables starting to shade.

  2. Stir in the mince and cook, breaking apart any clumps of meat with a wood spoon, until browned.

  3. Add the tomato purée, garlic and thyme, and cook dinner for 1-2 minutes more. Pour in the wine, if the use of the wine

    increases the heat to boil off maximum of the alcohol. Reduce the heat, stir inside the stock and season. Cover with a tight-fitting

    lid and leave to cook gently for 1 hr-1 hr 15 mins till the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened.

  4. Remove the lid and continue cooking for 15 mins. Meanwhile, cook the pasta following pack instructions. Reserve a mugful of the

    cooking water, then drain the spaghetti and add to the ragu with the Parmesan. Toss well and add a bit pasta water to assist the sauce

    coat the spaghetti. Serve with a side salad and extra cheese, if you like.
